CanSat Telemetry System Overview:

The CanSat is a student-built satellite that collects various telemetry data during flight. Key parameters include:

- Altitude: Measured in meters, typically ranges from ground level to target apogee
- Pressure: Atmospheric pressure in hPa, decreases with altitude
- Temperature: Ambient temperature in Celsius
- Voltage: Battery voltage, should remain above 3.0V for proper operation
- GPS Data: Latitude, longitude, and GPS altitude for position tracking
- Orientation: Roll, pitch, yaw angles from IMU sensors
- Flight States: Different phases of flight (pre-launch, ascent, descent, landed)
- Health Flags: System status indicators

Typical mission profile:
1. Pre-launch: System initialization and ground checks
2. Launch: Rapid ascent with increasing altitude
3. Apogee: Peak altitude, deployment of recovery systems
4. Descent: Parachute deployment and controlled descent
5. Landing: Touchdown and data transmission

Key performance metrics:
- Target altitude: Usually 300-500 meters for student competitions
- Flight duration: 5-15 minutes total
- Data transmission: Real-time telemetry via radio or cellular
- Recovery: GPS-guided recovery system

Safety considerations:
- Battery monitoring to prevent power loss
- Altitude limits to stay within FAA regulations
- Emergency recovery procedures
- Data logging for post-flight analysis
